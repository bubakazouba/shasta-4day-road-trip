Plan A — Lake Shasta (I-5 both ways)

The lake trip. Most driving, most pristine nature. 1,100 mi RT / ~17 drive-hours.
Day-by-day
  1. Day 1 (Fri) — Pasadena → Redding ~540 mi / 8–9h
  2. Day 2 (Sat) — Lake Shasta: Caverns + Houseboat on-lake
  3. Day 3 (Sun) — Mount Shasta + Dunsmuir loop ~130 mi loop
  4. Day 4 (Mon) — Shasta Dam + Drive home ~540 mi / 8–9h
Total drive: ~1,100 miles • ~17 hours behind the wheel over 4 days
Why I-5 both ways: fastest; keeps 2 days clear for actual lake time. A coastal return via 101 adds ~6 driving hours and another overnight — not worth it for a 4-day trip. Save the coast for a longer loop.
Best months: May–October for water activities; avoid December–February (lake levels + road closures).

Day 1 — Pasadena → Redding

Friday: get up the valley

540 mi8h30m w/o stopsLeave by 7am

Route

  • Pasadena → I-210 W → I-5 N. Stay on I-5 for the next 530 miles.
  • First long stretch: Grapevine → Central Valley. Cruise control country.

Suggested stops

  • Kettleman City (mile ~180) — Tesla Supercharger if EV, In-N-Out, gas. 1h15m from Pasadena.
  • Harris Ranch (mile ~210) — Classic I-5 steakhouse. Lunch at the Coalinga restaurant; drive-through gas station has its own bakery.
  • Olive Pit, Corning (mile ~480) — Stretch legs, olive + almond samples, cheesy roadside shop. Good coffee.
  • Sundial Bridge, Redding (arrival) — Calatrava-designed pedestrian bridge over the Sacramento River. 710ft span. Best photo spot in town at sunset.

Where to sleep

  • Sheraton Redding Hotel at the Sundial Bridge — ~$180/night, walking distance to the bridge + Turtle Bay park.
  • Tsasdi Resort (Lakehead, 20 min N of Redding) — Cabins right on Shasta Lake shoreline. ~$200/night, worth it if you want to wake up AT the lake.
  • Budget: Fairfield Inn / Best Western in Redding, ~$110/night.

Dinner

  • Moonstone Bistro (Redding) — farm-to-table, 4.5★, reservations needed on weekends.
  • Jack's Grill — decades-old steakhouse, cash-only classic, no reservations so go early.

Day 2 — Lake Shasta: Caverns + Houseboat

Saturday: the big lake day

Water dayBook both AM + PM ahead

Morning — Lake Shasta Caverns (10am tour)

  • Catamaran ride across the lake → bus up 800 ft → 1h cave tour with stalactites/stalagmites. Year-round, ~60°F inside.
  • Tickets: ~$38 adults, advance booking required on weekends. lakeshastacaverns.com
  • Tour total: ~2 hours door-to-door.

Afternoon — On-water

  • Option A (luxury): Houseboat day rental — Jones Valley, Silverthorn, or Bridge Bay. Sleeps 6–20, hot tubs, slides. Full week rentals start ~$3,500; day-use pontoons ~$500/day. Best booked 2026 season opens now.
  • Option B (simple): Pontoon rental from Holiday Harbor or Bridge Bay Marina. ~$395/half-day (4hrs), ~$595/full day. Captain yourself.
  • Option C (free): Swim + picnic at Jones Valley beach or Bailey Cove (accessible via short hike). Bring your own cooler.

Dinner

  • Bridge Bay Marina Restaurant — casual lake-side burgers + beers, patio over the water.
  • The Basshole (Lakehead) — dive-bar-with-food vibe, locals' spot.

Day 3 — Mount Shasta + Dunsmuir loop

Sunday: head north to the mountain

~130 mi round-tripI-5 N then back

Route stops (north to south)

  • Hedge Creek Falls, Dunsmuir (exit 730B) — 5-min trail from I-5 to a waterfall you can walk BEHIND. Literally off the freeway. Free.
  • Dunsmuir downtown — Railroad-era main street, coffee at Yaks or lunch at Cornerstone Bakery. Ted Fay Fly Shop if anyone fishes.
  • Lake Siskiyou Trail — 7-mile loop around a small lake with full Mt Shasta reflection. Or just walk the pedestrian bridge for the view (~20 min).
  • Mount Shasta City plaza — Small walkable downtown, Berryvale Grocery for organic picnic supplies, Mount Shasta City Park (headwaters of the Sacramento River — drink from the source).
  • Bunny Flat Trailhead (6,900 ft, ~30 min drive up Everitt Memorial Hwy) — Panoramic view of Mt Shasta, short easy hike into wildflower meadows (1-2 mi loops). Closed if snow; check conditions.

Back at Shasta Lake for dinner

  • Sunset from your cabin deck / hotel patio. Lake Shasta is southwest-facing, sunset hits Mt Shasta around 8–8:30pm in summer.

Day 4 — Shasta Dam + drive home

Monday: one quick morning stop, then back

540 mi8h30mLeave by 9am

Morning

  • Shasta Dam tour — 602 ft tall, 2nd-largest concrete dam in the US. Free guided tours M–F, 9am + 11am + 1pm (weekends 11am only). 1-hour tour inside the dam. Bring photo ID.
  • Turtle Bay Exploration Park (if skipping the dam) — River+animals+arboretum, ~2h visit.

The drive back (with Bakersfield stop)

  • Same I-5 in reverse. Target an early afternoon Bakersfield arrival for a proper break.
  • Bakersfield dinner stop (~mile 425 from Redding):
    • The Padre Hotel — historic downtown, Prairie Fire restaurant if you want a sit-down meal.
    • Luigi's Delicatessen — old-school Basque-Italian, a Bakersfield institution since 1910.
    • Pyrenees Cafe — Basque family-style dinner (lamb stew, sweetbreads). Full experience.
    • Kern County Museum if you want a quick 30-min stretch — outdoor historic village.
    • Gas in Bakersfield is cheaper than anywhere closer to Pasadena.
  • Grapevine can be slow 2–6pm on Mondays — aim to clear it by 4pm, or just commit to a later Bakersfield dinner and drive through after 7pm.
  • Expect ~9h with stops. Pasadena arrival ~7–8pm.

Plan B — Davis + PCH/SLO/Solvang loop

Up the valley, back down the coast. ~900 mi loop. Mix of college town + coastal drive.

Day 1 (Fri) — Pasadena → Davis

~430 mi / 6h30m

Straight I-5 up. Same Kettleman City + Harris Ranch lunch stop as Plan A. Arrive Davis mid-afternoon. Davis is a flat, bike-friendly UC college town with a laid-back downtown.

  • Arboretum walk — UC Davis runs a free 100-acre arboretum along Putah Creek. Easy 3-mile loop.
  • Downtown Davis — 3rd + E Streets. Dim sum at Burgers and Brew, coffee at Temple Coffee, ice cream at Davis Creamery.
  • Dinner: Osteria Fasulo (Italian, upscale) or Zen Toro (sushi). Both need reservations on a Friday.

Day 2 (Sat) — Davis + day trip option

Lots of directions to go from Davis:

  • Sacramento (20 min) — Capitol tour, Old Sac + railroad museum, Tower Bridge. Good lunch in Midtown.
  • Napa / Sonoma (1h west) — wine country. Stags Leap, Domaine Carneros, or ZD Wines. Limit to 2 tastings.
  • Lake Tahoe (2h east via I-80) — a stretch for a day trip but doable; south shore is the scenic pull-off.
  • Bodega Bay (2h15m west via Napa) — coastal fog + Hitchcock nostalgia if you want to get to the coast early.

Back to Davis for dinner + sleep.

Day 3 (Sun) — Davis → San Luis Obispo via coast

~340 mi / 6hMost scenic day

This is the long-scenic day. From Davis, cut west to the coast then follow 101 / PCH south.

  • Davis → I-80 W → I-680 S → US-101 S at Gilroy.
  • Carmel / Monterey (optional 30-min detour) — 17-mile drive, Cannery Row.
  • Big Sur stretch (~100 mi) — the iconic part. Bixby Bridge, McWay Falls, Pfeiffer Beach purple sand. Allow 3+ hours with photo stops.
  • Lunch at Nepenthe (Big Sur) — clifftop patio, famous view. Pricey but one-of-one.
  • Arrive SLO evening. Stay downtown.

Where to sleep: Granada Hotel (boutique, ~$260) or Apple Farm Inn (classic, ~$200). Budget: Motel 6 downtown, ~$95.

Day 4 (Mon) — SLO → Solvang → Pasadena

~220 mi / 4h without stops
  • SLO morning: Farmers market at Thursday nights only — on Monday just do downtown Higuera St + Bubblegum Alley photo stop. Breakfast at Kreuzberg or The Station Grill.
  • Solvang (45 min S of SLO) — the Danish-themed town. Windmills, pastries at Solvang Bakery or Olsen's Danish Village Bakery (aebleskiver — stuffed pancake balls). 2-hour walk is plenty.
  • Optional wine stop: Santa Ynez Valley has 120+ wineries. Sanford or Foxen are short drives from Solvang.
  • Back on 101 S → Pasadena. Arrive ~5–6pm.

Plan C — Pure coast: SLO + Solvang (short scenic)

If 4 days feels like too much driving, this is the lowest-stress option. ~550 mi RT, lots of beach + town time.

Day 1 (Fri) — Pasadena → Santa Barbara → Solvang

~170 mi / 3h
  • Leave late morning — this is a short drive day.
  • Santa Barbara — lunch on State St, Stearns Wharf, maybe the Old Mission.
  • Drive up over San Marcos Pass (154) into the Santa Ynez Valley.
  • Arrive Solvang by 4pm. Walk around before dinner.
  • Dinner: First & Oak (upscale) or Succulent Café (Mediterranean-Scandinavian fusion).
  • Sleep: Hotel Corque or Mirabelle Inn.

Day 2 (Sat) — Solvang + wine country

  • Aebleskiver breakfast (Olsen's or Solvang Restaurant).
  • Wineries: Sanford, Foxen, or Alma Rosa — pick 2 max. Or skip wine and visit Ostrich Land USA (yes, feed ostriches) on the way to wineries.
  • Chumash Casino nearby if that's your thing.
  • Dinner back in Solvang.

Day 3 (Sun) — Solvang → San Luis Obispo

~90 mi / 1h45m
  • Drive up 101 — go via Los Olivos (tiny wine town, 15 min from Solvang) for coffee.
  • Morro Bay (20 min past SLO) — climb the rock, fish tacos at Tognazzini's.
  • Afternoon in SLO — Bubblegum Alley, Mission Plaza, downtown wander.
  • Sunset at Avila Beach (20 min S of SLO) — quieter than Pismo, long pier.
  • Sleep SLO: same options as Plan B Day 3.

Day 4 (Mon) — SLO → Pasadena via PCH

~230 mi / 4h30m
  • Leave SLO late morning.
  • PCH drive: SLO → Pismo → Guadalupe Dunes → Santa Maria → Gaviota Coast → Santa Barbara → home.
  • Lunch at Mattei's Tavern (Los Olivos) or La Super-Rica Taqueria (Santa Barbara — Julia Child's favorite).
  • Arrive Pasadena ~6pm.

Plan D — Desert: Joshua Tree + Palm Springs + Salvation Mountain

The low-mileage, high-weird option. ~350 mi RT. Works any time Oct–April (too hot May–Sep).

Day 1 (Fri) — Pasadena → Palm Springs

~110 mi / 2h
  • Out I-10 E. Possible stop at Cabazon dinosaurs (Pee-wee's Big Adventure) for kitsch photos.
  • Check into Palm Springs hotel.
  • Afternoon: Palm Springs Aerial Tramway — 2.5-mile cable ride up 8,500 ft to San Jacinto peak. Brings you to alpine forest from desert in 10 min.
  • Dinner: Workshop Kitchen + Bar or Sandfish Sushi (trendy) / Elmer's (vintage).

Day 2 (Sat) — Joshua Tree all day

~90 mi loop
  • Enter park via 29 Palms (north entrance). $30/vehicle or free with Annual Pass.
  • Cholla Cactus Garden — dense teddy-bear cholla at sunrise (7am stop).
  • Keys View — Coachella Valley panorama.
  • Hidden Valley 1-mi loop — classic JT rock scramble.
  • Skull Rock + Split Rock — short hikes.
  • Pioneertown Mane St (just outside park) — Old West film set, lunch or dinner at Pappy & Harriet's (live music).
  • Return to Palm Springs for sleep.

Day 3 (Sun) — Salton Sea + Salvation Mountain

~200 mi loop from PS
  • Drive south on Hwy 86 along the Salton Sea (dying inland lake, surreal).
  • Salvation Mountain (Niland) — Leonard Knight's folk-art painted hillside. ~2h drive from Palm Springs. Free, donations welcome.
  • Slab City — off-grid squatter town adjacent. Anti-tourism but fascinating.
  • East Jesus — found-object sculpture garden.
  • Lunch at Red Earth Cafe (in Salton City) on the way.
  • Back to Palm Springs for dinner.

Day 4 (Mon) — Palm Springs morning → home

~110 mi
  • Morning: Desert X (sculpture park, runs Feb–Apr even years), or Moorten Botanical Garden, or hike Tahquitz Canyon.
  • Lunch at Cheeky's (brunch classic, arrive before 11).
  • Drive home, arrive Pasadena ~4pm.

Plan E — Eastern Sierra: Mammoth + Mono Lake + Bodie

Alpine + desert + ghost town. ~700 mi RT. Works May–October.

Day 1 (Fri) — Pasadena → Mammoth Lakes

~320 mi / 5h30m
  • Out US-395 N (via 14 through Mojave).
  • Red Rock Canyon State Park — roadside Mars-like canyons, 10-min stretch.
  • Fossil Falls — off-395 volcanic rock formations.
  • Manzanar National Historic Site — WWII Japanese incarceration camp, powerful 1-hour visit.
  • Arrive Mammoth evening. Sleep at Westin Monache or Mammoth Mountain Inn.

Day 2 (Sat) — Mammoth Lakes + Devils Postpile

  • Mammoth Gondola to 11,053 ft summit.
  • Devils Postpile + Rainbow Falls — hexagonal basalt columns, 5-mi RT hike.
  • Mammoth Lakes Basin — kayak Lake Mary, bike the paved loop.
  • Dinner at 53 Kitchen & Cocktails or The Mogul (steakhouse).

Day 3 (Sun) — Mono Lake + Bodie ghost town

~100 mi loop N of Mammoth
  • Mono Lake South Tufa — ancient alkaline lake with limestone spires. 1-mile loop at sunrise for best light.
  • Bodie State Historic Park — preserved 1880s gold-rush ghost town, 170 buildings in "arrested decay." 13-mile dirt road off 395 (fine in regular car).
  • Lunch at Mono Lake Committee info center (cafe) or Virginia Creek Settlement.
  • Back to Mammoth for dinner.

Day 4 (Mon) — Mammoth → Pasadena via 395

~320 mi
  • Stop at Alabama Hills (Lone Pine) — movie-set boulders with Mt Whitney backdrop. 1h easy loops (Mobius Arch trail).
  • Lunch at Alabama Hills Café (Lone Pine) — massive pancakes.
  • Continue down 395 → 14 → 5 → Pasadena. Arrive ~7pm.

Plan F — Sequoia + Kings Canyon + Bakersfield

Giant trees day-by-day. ~500 mi RT. Summer or fall.

Day 1 (Fri) — Pasadena → Three Rivers (Sequoia gateway)

~220 mi / 4h
  • I-5 N → CA-99 → CA-198 E into the foothills.
  • Check into cabin in Three Rivers (Rio Sierra Riverhouse, Buckeye Tree Lodge).
  • Dinner on Kaweah River patio.

Day 2 (Sat) — Sequoia NP

  • General Sherman Tree — world's largest (by volume) living tree. 1-mi walk.
  • Congress Trail — 2-mi easy loop through the sequoia grove.
  • Moro Rock — 350-step granite dome with 360° view. Epic.
  • Crystal Cave — guided tour, book ahead.
  • Tunnel Log — drive through a fallen sequoia.

Day 3 (Sun) — Kings Canyon NP

~60 mi from Three Rivers
  • Drive up CA-180 into Kings Canyon — Sierra scenic drive.
  • General Grant Tree (2nd largest).
  • Zumwalt Meadow 1.5-mi loop (Kings River + canyon walls).
  • Roaring River Falls roadside stop.
  • Back to Three Rivers.

Day 4 (Mon) — Bakersfield dinner → Pasadena

~220 mi via Bakersfield
  • Leave morning. CA-198 → CA-99 S → Bakersfield.
  • Late lunch / early dinner in Bakersfield — Luigi's Deli or Pyrenees Cafe (Basque).
  • Over the Grapevine → Pasadena. Arrive ~7pm.

Packing list

  • Water: swimsuit, towel, reef-safe sunscreen, sunglasses, wide-brim hat.
  • Cave: light sweater (60°F inside the caverns).
  • Hiking: running shoes or light hikers, water bottle, light jacket (Mt Shasta elevation is chilly even in summer).
  • Dam tour: photo ID (required to enter).
  • Car: phone mount for navigation (cell service patchy past Redding), snacks, paper map as backup, jumper cables, tire pressure check before leaving.
  • Camera: spare battery — best light for Mt Shasta is early AM + sunset.

Booking checklist (do today)
